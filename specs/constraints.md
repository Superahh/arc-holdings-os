# Constraints

## Legal and safety constraints

- No illegal behavior.
- No harmful behavior.
- No fraudulent or deceptive workflow assumptions.
- No bypassing marketplace, payment, or platform rules.

## Business realism constraints

- No magical money-generation assumptions.
- No fake certainty on valuation, repair outcomes, or resale timing.
- Assume operational friction exists.
- Treat fees, shipping, labor, and risk as real.

## Product constraints

- Keep version 1 narrow.
- Preserve modular architecture.
- Keep outputs implementation-ready.
- Favor reusable structure over one-off hacks.
- Make non-goals explicit.

## Automation constraints

- Human approval is required for risky actions in version 1.
- Do not assume unsupervised purchasing or listing.
- Automation must be tightly constrained, auditable, and justified.
- Low-capital behavior must remain within approved strategy classes and must not trigger unbounded autonomous monetization behavior.

## Integration constraints

- The system must not depend on the user manually supplying API keys during normal use.
- Prefer internal or mocked data paths for early planning and prototyping.
- Avoid broad integrations in version 1.

## UX constraints

- Keep the interface ADHD-friendly.
- Keep sections and screens clear and low-clutter.
- Every visual element must map to real business state.
- The office simulation must support decision-making, not distract from it.

## Scope constraints

- Do not overengineer version 1.
- Avoid decorative game mechanics without operational value.
- Avoid building a full ERP, accounting suite, or warehouse platform.
